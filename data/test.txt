Sepsis is a life-threatening condition caused by the body's response to an infection.
The immune system releases chemicals into the bloodstream to fight infection, but these
chemicals can cause widespread inflammation. Symptoms of sepsis include fever, chills,
rapid breathing, rapid heart rate, and confusion. Early detection and treatment with
antibiotics and fluids is critical for survival. Septic shock is the most severe form
of sepsis and can cause organ failure and death.
